YOUR WORKSPACE WALKTHROUGH

    WEBSITE PREREQUISITE

  • How do I enable my outlook link?
    •    To Enable Outlook Links follow these Procedures:

         Links in emails messages are turned off by default in MS Outlook for security reasons.
         Worms, viruses, and the likes have been known to lure unsuspecting users to malicious sites through active links in spam messages.
         However, in order for you to utilize the APPROVE and DECLINE links in the email notices in workflow, you have to enable the links in the email messages.
         When you receive an email message from workflow, Outlook prompts you with a warning message just above the email itself about a link in email body.
         You can click on this warning message and then choose "enable links from this site".

  • How do I setup Trusted site zone?
    •    The trusted zone contains websites that you trust will not damage your computer or data.
         Such websites can be found in your organization's intranet or established companies in whom you have confidence.
         You can configure your security settings to a trusted zone by following these steps:

         1. Click on the Internet Explorer, on the Tools menu, click Internet Options.
         2. On the Security Tab, select a web content zone to specify your security level. In this context, click on the trusted sites zone.
         3. Locate Security level for this zone header to customize your security setting based on your specified zone.

  • How do I enable automatic logon on internet?
    •    Modifying your security setting will allow users and administrator to acquire
         more control over all security options. You can configure the security setting by following these steps:

         1. Click on the Internet Explorer, on the Tools menu, click Internet Options.
         2. On the Security Tab, click on the Custom Level. After this action, a security setting window will appear.
         3. In the Security setting box, locate User Authentication heading   located at the bottom, and select Automatic Logon only in Internet zone radio button. This option prompts users for user IDs     and passwords in other zones.
         4. Click Ok button. A message box will appear verifying the changes you made.
         5. Click YES button to apply these changes. After this action, security setting window will close and redirect you to the security page.
         6. Click Ok button to exit the window.

  • How do I configure Proxy setting in IE?
    •    To access the information on your internal network, you need to configure your proxy setting.
         To do this, follow these steps:

         1. In the Internet Explorer,on the tool menu, click Internet Options.
         2. Click Connection tab then locate the Local Area Network (LAN) Setting section.
         3. Click on the LAN Setting.
         4. In the proxy server section, locate the checkbox named as "User proxy server for your LAN" and check the box.
         5. In the address field, key in the proxy address: 142.130.2.43
         6. In the port field, key in the proxy field: 3128
         7. Locate the checkbox named as "Bypass proxy server for local address" and check the box.
         8. Click OK to exit LAN setting window.

  • How do I enable cookies in IE?
    •    Cookies are used to preserve the state of information as you return to or navigate through different pages of a websites.
         and is required by the workflow application. To enable them do the following:

         1. Clicking Internet Option on the Tools menu, and then clicking on the Privacy Tab.
         2. You will see the privacy slider tab, which contains six settings:
             Block All Cookies, High, Medium High, Medium (Default Level), Low, and Accept All Cookies.
         3. Move your slider to Accept All Cookies.
         4. Locate the Pop-up Blocker header to customize your pop-ups setting.

  • How do I enable Pop-ups?
    •    Pop-up messages are required by the workflow application for confirmation messages on the different action buttons.
         To enable your pop-ups follow these steps:

         1. Clicking Internet Option on the Tools menu, and then clicking on the Privacy Tab.
         2. Locate Pop-up Blocker Header.
         3. Locate the Checkbox named as Block pop-ups and uncheck the box.
         4. Click Ok button to exit the window.


    • SUBMITTING A REQUEST

  •  How do I submit a workflow request?
    •    The workflow main page has a list of all available processes that is in production. You can select from the list and it will bring up the submission form for that particular process.
         Once you have submitted it, the request will be sent to the designated approver for the necessary action.

  •  Can I save the request in a draft form?
    •    Yes, you can. By clicking the SAVE DRAFT button, you can save the current Request as a Draft
         and the request would be saved and then be available on your personal worklist.

  •  Do I need to follow up approvers?
    •    No, you don't need to follow up approvers.
         Notifications will automaticaly be sent to the approvers reminding them to take necessary actions on the request.

  •  How do I check the status of a request that I submitted?
    •    The workflow system will send you an email notification whenever your request gets routed to the next step.
         Alternatively, you can also check for the status of all or any of your pending requests through the search facility of the ENP Workspace.

  •  Will I be notified of the progress of my request?
    •    Yes, notifications will be sent to you by the following events:

         1. When you submit a request.
         2. When an approver appproves your request Approval.
         3. When an approver requests for additional information.
         4. When an approver declines your request.
         5. When an approver approves your request.


    • WORKING WITH MY ENP WORKSPACE

  •  How do I access my ENP Workspace?
    •    You can access your ENP Workspace by following these steps:

         1. Go to the ENPAP corporate intranet website (http://hub.enpap/default.aspx)
         2. Click the workflow link on the main page.
         3. Click on the link "ENP Workspace"
         4. A pop-up window will prompt you to enter your Username and Password for validation. Enter your Username and Password.

         Only the prescribed domain accounts have been given access rights to the workflow system, therefore all employees must use only the individual accounts in the "enp-ap\firstname.lastname" format.
         If you are unable to login or encounter any account validation issues, please contact the Workflow administrator (workflow.admin@emersonnetowk-ap.com).

  •  How do I log using the network domain account?
    •    Using their ENP Workspace domain credentials, an authorized user can access the workspace.
         The system is designed to use integrated windows authentication, user will be prompted to login their account to confirm if he/she is a member of the authorized users.

         Enter your Username and Password as provided by IT, username will be in the format of enp-ap\username. Click the OK button.
         * If you are unable to login or encounter any account validation issues, please contact the Workflow administrator (workflow.admin@emersonnetowk-ap.com).

         Once authenticated, you will be redirected the ENP Workspace.
         The Workspace by default will display the Worklist section. The Worklist is divided into two parts:
         The Worklist Options, located at the left part this will serve as a request filter, while the Worklist Items, located at the right part will contain all items pending your action.

  •  What is process filter for?
    •    The process filter gives you the flexibility of limiting the view of active requests that are pending your action.
         By default, the system detects which processes you have rights to and lists them here for your convenience.

  •  How do I sort my worklist items?
    •    Users can sort requests by clicking one of the column headers.



  •  Can I save frequently used searches, ie favorites?
    •    The User can search a request using the search field, filter based on status, and process type.
         The search field allows you to use wild character criteria such as:

         *Keyword
         Keyword*

      *    Placed "*" before the keyword to search all request that has the keyword at the beginning.
      *    Placed "*" after the keyword to search all request that has the keyword at the end.

      For frequently used searches, you can opt to save each particular search criteria and it will be added to your saved searches combo box.
      You can then simply pick one at any time and then click SEARCH.


    • WORKING ON A PENDING REQUEST

  •  How do I put comments or remarks?
    •    This section is used whenever you wish to add any free form text on a particular request.
         You can fill in comments and attach files as may be needed.

         1. Place your remarks here.
         
         2. Attach your files relevant to the request here.
         

  •  How do I approve a request?
    •    From within the request form, navigate down to the action buttons and click Approve.
         The system will pop up a confirmation message before finally sending the item off to the next step.

  •  How do I decline a request?
    •    From within the request form, navigate down to the action buttons and click Decline.
         The system will pop up a confirmation message before finally sending the item off to the workflow archive.


  •  If I need clarifications, can I send this request back to the initiator?
    •    Yes, you can. If you feel that the request has insufficient information, you can solicit additional information from the initiator.
         From within the request form, navigate down to the action buttons and click Request Info.

  •  What are the forward options?
    •    This functionality allows you to forward the request to somebody else for approval or just send a notification email to someone as an FYI
         It is important to note that the workflow system already has the built-in logic to intelligently route this request to the necessary people as defined in the process.
         The Forward for Approval option however allows you to explicitly add 1 more person to this routing matrix.

         The Send FYI option allows you to send email notices to multiple people about this particular request.
         It does not however give them the right to approve or decline the request, nor does it grant them view/read rights to this request within the workflow system.

  •  Can I print the request information?
    •    Yes, you can. From within the request form, navigate down to the acton buttons and click Print Prev button.
         After which the system will pop up an appropriation request form dislaying all relevant details of the request,
         navigate down to the action buttons and click Print button.

  •  Can I cancel a request?
    •    Yes, you can. To cancel a request, you will need to search for the request you want to cancel. From the workspace
         navigate down to the menu buttons and click Search. After which the system will direct you to a search page,
         use the search options to look for the request. Select the request from the search results,
         the system will then pop up the request details and on the upper part of the page, click the cancel link.

         Note that only the initiator will be given rights to cancel the request he/she created.

  •  What is the system info tab for?
    •    System info is used to display relevant details about the specific request that would be useful for the users.
         This is divided into 3 sections: audit trail, system internals and route diagram.

         The Audit Trail will contain a running record of event and actions associated with this request.

  •  What is the audit trail for?
    •    The System Internals shows some related information on the process and the form, and who the process owner is.
      You can also see a visual representation of the process through the route diagram.

  •  What is the system internals for?
    •    The System Internals shows some related information on the process and the form, and who the process owner is.
         You can also see a visual representation of the process through the route diagram by clicking this button.
